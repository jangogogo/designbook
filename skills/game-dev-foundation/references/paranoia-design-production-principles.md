# Paranoia-Derived Design Production Principles

Adapted from `DY-2026/ParanoiaSkills`.

## Operating Principles

- Evidence before opinion.
- Feasibility before scope.
- Workflow before one-off prompts.
- Value of information before research.
- Eval before promotion.
- Rollback before confidence.

## Concept To Validation

Turn an idea into:

- concept seed
- player verbs
- action-goal alignment
- player promise
- core loop
- design nucleus options
- scope gate
- assumption ledger
- prototype validation plan

Do not let a concept remain only a mood or theme. Every promising direction needs a playable promise and a validation gate.

## Evidence-First Analysis

When reviewing a game, prototype, trailer, or recording:

- Ground claims in screenshots, timestamps, direct observations, metrics, or playtest notes.
- Separate evidence, inference, and recommendation.
- Convert issues into testable cards: symptom, evidence, suspected cause, priority, fix direction, validation.
- Avoid declaring transferability from another game until sample boundaries are clear.

## Experience Density

For first-session, retention, pacing, feedback, embodiment, atmosphere, or cognitive-load problems:

- Diagnose the actual low-density moment.
- Identify whether the gap is clarity, feedback, agency, rhythm, reward, surprise, or overload.
- Propose small weekly variants rather than broad redesign.
- Define instrumentation, dashboard fields, pass/fail decision rules, and rollback.

## Workflow Evolution

When promoting a useful habit into a rule:

- Define the desired behavior.
- State why it matters now.
- Identify risks and rollback.
- Add a small eval or realistic test prompt.
- Keep the rule portable and not overfit to one conversation.

## Public / Private Boundary

- Keep real private project details local unless explicitly intended for publication.
- Public examples should be synthetic, public, cleared, or marked for review.
- Do not push private client/project traces into public skill packages.

## Useful Output Forms

For design work, prefer compact, reviewable artifacts:

- one-page concept brief
- player promise contract
- assumption ledger
- validation plan
- vertical slice plan
- issue cards
- experience-density experiment plan
- milestone gate plan
